01 The Problem

Recruiters sift through hundreds of profiles but still miss the right person — because keyword filters can't see what actually matters.

Traditional Keyword Matching

  • Matches "Python" but misses context
  • Can't detect career trajectory quality
  • Ignores behavioral availability signals
  • Fooled by keyword stuffers and fake profiles
  • Binary match → no nuanced ranking
→

Our Hybrid AI Approach

  • Semantic similarity understands intent
  • Career trajectory + company type analysis
  • 8-dimensional behavioral scoring
  • 5-strategy honeypot & stuffer detection
  • Continuous scores → trustworthy ranking
Challenge: Rank 100,000 candidates for the Senior AI Engineer (Founding Team) role. Deliver a top-100 shortlist a recruiter can trust — in under 5 minutes, on CPU only, with no network access.

04 Semantic Scoring — TF-IDF

Why TF-IDF over Transformer Embeddings?

The compute constraint (CPU-only, 5 minutes, 16GB RAM) rules out transformer models for 100K candidates. TF-IDF with scikit-learn processes the entire corpus in 67 seconds and captures domain-specific bigrams like "vector search", "hybrid retrieval", "sentence transformers" effectively.

How It Works

  • Document Construction: For each candidate, concatenate headline, summary, career descriptions, skill names, certifications, and education into a single text document
  • Vectorization: TfidfVectorizer with (1,2)-grams, sublinear TF, 8000 max features
  • Scoring: Cosine similarity between JD vector and each candidate vector
  • Normalization: Min-max scaling to [0,1] to stretch the naturally narrow similarity range
What we'd do with more compute: Use sentence-transformers (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) for dense embeddings, add BM25 for hybrid retrieval (sparse + dense), and a cross-encoder reranker for the top 500.

08 Reasoning Generation

Each candidate gets a unique, fact-checked justification. No hallucination — every claim is verified against the profile data.

Design Principles

  • Fact-grounded: Only states data that exists in the candidate's profile
  • Rank-aware: Top-25 get confident language, bottom-40 acknowledge concerns
  • Varied: Template bank with deterministic randomization → 100% unique (100/100)
  • Honest: Acknowledges concerns like "long notice period" or "low response rate"

10 Design Tradeoffs

Why Hybrid over Pure Semantic?

Pure semantic matching can't capture structural signals the JD explicitly mentions: company type, experience range, career stability, notice period. Pure rule-based misses candidates who describe work differently. The hybrid approach captures both.

Why Title > Skills?

The JD warns: "A candidate who lists all the AI keywords but whose title is Marketing Manager is not a fit." Title is a more trustworthy signal of what someone actually does than a self-reported skill list.

Why Behavioral as Separate (not Combined)?

Behavioral measures availability, which is orthogonal to fit. A great engineer with a 120-day notice period and 5% response rate is a great engineer — but not a hireable one right now.

What Changes with GPU + API?

With more compute: sentence-transformers for dense embeddings, BM25 + dense hybrid retrieval, cross-encoder reranking for top 500, LLM reasoning generation instead of templates. This is what the Senior AI Engineer role would build at Redrob.
